# HR Employee Attrition Analysis — Excel

## Overview
This project analyzes employee attrition data from IBM's HR Analytics dataset containing 1470 employees and 35 variables. The goal was to identify key drivers of employee turnover and present findings in a clear executive ready dashboard.

## Dataset
IBM HR Analytics Employee Attrition Dataset
1470 rows and 35 columns after cleaning

## Tools Used
Microsoft Excel — data cleaning, pivot tables, formulas, dashboard

## Process
- Imported and reviewed raw data
- Cleaned dataset by removing irrelevant columns
- Built pivot tables to analyze attrition by department, job role, overtime, job level and monthly income
- Calculated attrition rates using COUNTIF and manual percentage formulas
- Built interactive dashboard with KPI cards and charts

## Key Findings
- Overall company attrition rate is 16%
- Sales department has the highest attrition at 21%
- Sales Representative role has the most alarming attrition at 40%
- Employees working overtime leave at 31% versus 10% for non overtime workers
- Entry level employees leave at 26% compared to 5 to 7% for senior levels

## Dashboard Highlights
- 4 KPI cards showing headline attrition numbers
- 5 charts showing attrition by department, job role, overtime, job level and monthly income
- Clean professional layout with color coded warning indicators

## Business Recommendations
- Investigate workload and compensation for Sales Representatives immediately
- Review overtime policies as they are strongly correlated with attrition
- Build retention programs targeting entry level employees in year one and two
